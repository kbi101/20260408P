# Research Specification 011: Equilibrium Phases (Sideways)

This document defines the high-density consolidation patterns that signify a market in "Equilibrium." These patterns represent energy being coiled for a future expansion (breakout).

---

## 1. The Bollinger Band Squeeze
**Definition:** Occurs when volatility reaches a historical minimum, causing the bands to contract tightly around the price.
*   **Logic:** BandWidth = (Upper Band - Lower Band) / Middle Band. Match if current BandWidth is within 15% of the 6-month historical minimum.
*   **Significance:** Volatility is a cycle; low volatility invariably leads to high volatility. The Squeeze is the "calm before the storm."

---

## 2. Darvas Box (Rectangle Consolidation)
**Definition:** Price is bound between a rigid ceiling (Resistance) and floor (Support) with no clear directional trend.
*   **Logic:** Price remains within a tight horizontal box (height < 6%) for at least 20 periods.
*   **Significance:** Indicates significant accumulation or distribution before a major trend leg.

![Darvas Box Archetype](/assets/patterns/darvas_box.png)

---

## 3. Low ADX (Trend Exhaustion)
**Definition:** The Average Directional Index (ADX) falls below 20, signifying a complete lack of trending momentum.
*   **Logic:** ADX(14) < 20. 
*   **Significance:** This is the regime for mean-reversion and oscillators. Trend-following models should be sidelined until ADX expansions resume.
